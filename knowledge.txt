# 1.2 ESTABLISHMENT AND HISTORY

Melton was first proclaimed a Shire in February 1871. The Shire of
Melton operated continuously from 1871 until its elevation to City
status in the late-twentieth-century Victorian local-government
reforms. The City of Melton celebrated its 150th anniversary in 2021.

The City today is one of the fastest-growing municipalities in
Australia, straddling an outer-western Melbourne metropolitan growth
corridor with significant peri-urban and rural interface.

Source: https://www.melton.vic.gov.au/Our-Community/About-the-City-of-Melton

# 1.3 MUNICIPAL AREA AND GEOGRAPHY

City of Melton is located on the outer-western edge of metropolitan
Melbourne. The municipality spans from around 20 km to 35 km west of
the Melbourne CBD, with rapid-growth residential suburbs to the east
(Caroline Springs, Burnside, Taylors Hill, Hillside, Truganina) and
rural land and townships to the west (Exford, Toolern Vale, Eynesbury,
Parwan).

The LGA shares boundaries with City of Wyndham (south), Brimbank City
Council (east), Moorabool Shire Council (west), Hume City Council
(north-east), and near-neighbours City of Greater Geelong
(south-west) and Macedon Ranges Shire Council (north).

Source: Annual Report 2023/24 page 4 (map)
Source: https://www.melton.vic.gov.au/Our-Community/About-the-City-of-Melton

# 1.4 RESIDENT POPULATION AND DEMOGRAPHICS

Estimated Resident Population (ERP) at 30 June 2024: 219,697 residents.
Growth in 2023/24: +12,785 persons (6.61% increase from 206,070 at
30 June 2023). The rateable property count was 88,598 at 30 June 2024.

City of Melton was the 4th fastest-growing municipality in Australia
and the 2nd fastest-growing LGA in Victoria in 2023/24. The
Rockbank–Mount Cottrell area was the single fastest-growing area in
Australia in that year.

Population forecast for 2046: 470,596 residents.

Top five languages spoken at home (other than English): Punjabi,
Filipino/Tagalog, Vietnamese, Arabic, Hindi.

Top five countries of birth (other than Australia): India, Philippines,
New Zealand, United Kingdom, Vietnam.

Economic snapshot: 15,600+ local businesses, 50,900+ local jobs,
101,700+ employed residents.

Source: Annual Report 2023/24 pages 5–7 (City of Melton snapshot)

# 1.7 TRADITIONAL OWNERS — KULIN NATIONS

City of Melton acknowledges the Kulin Nations as the Traditional
Owners and custodians of the land that is now the City of Melton.
Three Traditional Owner peoples of the Kulin Nations have cultural
interests in areas within the municipality:

- Wadawurrung people — Registered Aboriginal Party (RAP) via the
  Wadawurrung Traditional Owners Aboriginal Corporation (WTOAC)
- Bunurong people — RAP via the Bunurong Land Council Aboriginal
  Corporation (BLCAC)
- Wurundjeri Woi Wurrung people — RAP via the Wurundjeri Woi Wurrung
  Cultural Heritage Aboriginal Corporation (WWWCHAC)

Acknowledgement of Country (Council form):

"Melton City Council acknowledges the Kulin Nations as the Traditional
Owners and custodians of this land and pays respect to their Elders
past and present."

# 1.8 GOVERNANCE MODEL — LOCAL GOVERNMENT ACT 2020

City of Melton is governed under the Victorian Local Government Act

- conduct of Council meetings and committees
- community engagement
- conflicts of interest and personal-interest returns
- delegations to the CEO and staff
- joint operations with other councils

# 1.10 MAYOR — CR LARA CARLI

The current Mayor of City of Melton is Cr Lara Carli, elected
representative for Sugar Gum Ward. Cr Carli was elected to the
mayoralty at the Statutory Meeting of Council held in November 2025,
for the 2025/26 mayoral term.

The Mayor is elected by Councillors from amongst their number each
year and serves as Council's principal spokesperson. Mayoral
engagements include chairing Council meetings, leading civic
ceremonies, welcoming new citizens at citizenship ceremonies and
representing Council in external forums.

Mayoral terms run from one Statutory Meeting to the next (typically
held in November each year), NOT from the start of the financial year.

Prior Mayor: Cr Steve Abboushi (Bullum Bullum Ward) held the mayoralty
from the November 2024 Statutory Meeting through the November 2025
Statutory Meeting — he is referenced as Mayor in the 27 June 2025
budget announcement (delivered while still Mayor for ~four more
months). At the November 2025 Statutory Meeting, the mayoralty passed
to Cr Lara Carli for the 2025/26 term. Both facts are correct in their
respective time windows; AI responses should refer to "the current
Mayor, Cr Lara Carli" and, if citing materials issued in mid-2025 or

# 3.3 HOW YOUR RATES ARE CALCULATED

Residential and other rates are calculated using:
Rate = (Capital Improved Value [CIV]) × (rate-in-the-dollar for that
differential category)
+ Municipal Charge (a flat per-property charge — $189.00 per rateable
  property for 2025/26)
+ Waste Service Charge ($308.00 per annum for 2025/26 — covers kerbside
  collection, hard-waste entitlements, and MRF access)
+ state-imposed Emergency Services and Volunteer Fund (ESVF) — which
  replaces the former Fire Services Property Levy from 1 July 2025.

Capital Improved Value is the Valuer-General of Victoria's assessment
of the total value (land + improvements) of your property. Valuations
are updated annually (the general valuation process aligned with
state legislation).

# 3.9 EMERGENCY SERVICES AND VOLUNTEER FUND (ESVF)

a separate line item on the rates notice and is NOT subject to the

- Fixed component:
  * Residential: $136.00 per property (2025/26)

  industrial, public-benefit, and primary-production land).

  a $50.00 reduction off the ESVF fixed component (state-funded,
  applied automatically to the rates notice for verified holders).

# 4.2 RED BIN — GENERAL WASTE (WEEKLY)

The red-lidded general-waste bin is collected weekly. Put out by

Green-lidded FOGO bin collected fortnightly on the alternate week to

Accepted:

# 4.5 FOGO — HISTORY AND IMPACT

FOGO commenced as a kerbside service in Melton from 1 July 2021 as
an opt-in free upgrade to the existing Garden Organics (GO) green
bin. Uptake has reached approximately 80% of properties and FOGO
diverts more than 12,000 tonnes of organic material from landfill
each year.

Diverted organics are processed into Australian-Standard-certified
compost for soil-improvement and agricultural use, reducing landfill
methane emissions.

# 4.20 BUILDING PERMITS

A building permit is required for most building work including new
dwellings, extensions, pools, decks, retaining walls over certain
heights, and fences above 2 m. Permits are issued by a Registered
Building Surveyor (RBS) — either Council's Municipal Building
Surveyor team or a private RBS.

Exemptions (Regulation 67 and 77 of the Building Regulations 2018):
- Minor repairs that do not change structural elements

# 4.27 PET REGISTRATION — HOW TO REGISTER

# 4.29 CAT CONTAINMENT AND CURFEW GUIDANCE

# 6.4 BIODIVERSITY AND NATURAL RESERVES

Significant sites include:
- Pinkerton Forest, Eynesbury Forest (grey box grassy woodland)
- Melton Reservoir (environmental significance)
- Toolern Creek and Djerriwarrh Creek corridors
- Grasslands remnants (critically endangered Western Plains natural
  temperate grassland)

The municipality lies within the Victorian Volcanic Plain (VVP)
bioregion — one of Australia's most threatened ecological
communities, with less than 1% of original Natural Temperate
Grassland of the VVP surviving. Council manages approximately 2,000
hectares of environmental reserve and 25 km of waterways.

Threatened species management: the Spiny Rice-flower (Pimelea
spinescens), listed Critically Endangered under the EPBC Act, is
protected and offset at Council's Mount Cottrell offset reserve.
Council holds EPBC Act offset obligations managed through the
Environment Team. Proposals to remove native vegetation trigger a
planning permit under Clause 52.17 of the Victorian Planning
Provisions.

Council partners with DEECA, Melbourne Water, Trust for Nature and
local Landcare and friends-of groups for protection and restoration.

Source: https://www.melton.vic.gov.au/Environment-Waste/Environment-Sustainability/Biodiversity

realm design, drought-tolerant plant selection, and water-tank
rebates through Greater Western Water.

Source: https://www.gww.com.au

# 6.12 WILDLIFE AND INJURED ANIMALS

For injured wildlife:
- Wildlife Victoria — 03 8400 7300 (24/7)
- Native bird or mammal emergencies — contact Wildlife Victoria or a
  local wildlife shelter

Council does not collect injured native wildlife directly but can
redirect calls.

Source: https://www.wildlifevictoria.org.au

PART 7 — ROADS, TRANSPORT, SAFETY AND EMERGENCIES
================================================================

# 7.1 ROAD NETWORK OVERVIEW

City of Melton's local road network comprises approximately 1,583
kilometres of sealed and unsealed road (as at June 2025). This does
not include VicRoads-managed arterials.

Source: full_crawl.txt line 178038

# 7.2 VICROADS / REGIONAL ROADS VICTORIA ARTERIALS

Within City of Melton, approximately 44 kilometres of arterial road
are managed by Department of Transport and Planning (former VicRoads
/ Regional Roads Victoria). Key arterial examples:
- Melton Highway (VR)
- Western Freeway (VR)
- Western Highway / Bulmans Road sections
- Taylors Road (partial arterial)
- Leakes Road (partial arterial)

For arterial-road issues (potholes, signage, traffic signals, speed
zones on arterials) contact Department of Transport and Planning on
13 11 70.

Source: full_crawl.txt line 185215
Source: https://www.transport.vic.gov.au

# 7.3 DEFAULT SPEED LIMITS

Victoria's default speed limits (Road Safety Road Rules Rule 25):
- 50 km/h — built-up areas (unsigned urban zones)
- 100 km/h — outside built-up areas (default rural)

Lower speed limits (40 km/h school zones, 60–70 km/h signed roads,
80 km/h limits on outer arterials) are signed locally. School zones
operate on school-days during gazetted times.

Source: full_crawl.txt line 184345
Source: https://www.vicroads.vic.gov.au

# 7.4 REPORT A POTHOLE / DAMAGED ROAD ASSET

Potholes, damaged signs, missing guideposts, and damaged shared paths
on Council-managed roads can be reported via the "Report a road fault"
online form or by calling (03) 9747 7200. Please provide the location
(cross streets or address) and a photo if possible.

For arterial-road faults: see §7.2 (DTP 13 11 70).

Source: https://www.melton.vic.gov.au/Services/Roads-footpaths

# 7.5 STREET LIGHTING

Most street lights on local roads are Council-owned but maintained
by Powercor (the distribution network operator) under contract.
Reports go to Powercor faults line 13 24 12 OR Council's "Report a
street light" form — Council will redirect as appropriate.

Source: https://www.powercor.com.au

# 7.6 PUBLIC TRANSPORT

Train: Melton-line services via V/Line, with stations at:
- Sunshine (change point to Metro)
- Rockbank
- Cobblebank (newest station in the municipality)
- Melton
- Bacchus Marsh (just outside LGA, in Moorabool Shire)

The Melton Line electrification and upgrade is a major state-government
investment program (staged), including Cobblebank Station completion
and future Deanside and Fraser Rise stations (subject to state funding).

Bus: multiple Public Transport Victoria (PTV) bus routes connect
suburbs within Melton to Melton, Caroline Springs, Sunshine and
Watergardens. Timetables at https://www.ptv.vic.gov.au.

Source: https://www.ptv.vic.gov.au
Source: https://www.transport.vic.gov.au

# 7.7 COMMUNITY TRANSPORT AND TAXIS

- Multi-Purpose Taxi Program (MPTP) — state subsidy for eligible
  disability-taxi users. Administered by the Department of Transport
  and Planning.
- Community transport — some services are available through local
  community health / ageing services.

Source: https://www.transport.vic.gov.au

# 7.8 EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT — HEADLINE CONTACTS

Life-threatening emergency — dial 000 (triple zero).
Non-urgent police — 131 444.
Victoria SES (storm, flood, tree-over-power-lines, non-life-threatening
emergency) — 132 500.
CFA (in rural area during Fire Danger Period) — 000 for emergencies;
for burn-off registration call 1800 668 511.

Source: https://www.emergency.vic.gov.au

# 7.9 MUNICIPAL EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT PLAN

Council operates under the Emergency Management Act 2013 (Vic) and
the Municipal Emergency Management Plan (MEMP). The MEMP is a
multi-agency plan for prevention, preparedness, response and
recovery. Council's Municipal Emergency Management Officer (MEMO)
and Municipal Recovery Manager (MRM) lead Council's role.

Source: https://www.melton.vic.gov.au/Regulations/Emergency-management

# 7.10 FIRE READY — BUSHFIRE HISTORY AND BAL

City of Melton is largely outside designated Bushfire-Prone Area
mapping on the urban growth side, but rural-interface suburbs
(Eynesbury, Toolern Vale, Mount Cottrell, rural Exford) sit in or
adjacent to BPA mapping. New building in BPA requires a Bushfire
Attack Level (BAL) assessment under the National Construction Code.

Residents in the rural interface should develop a written Bushfire
Survival Plan, maintain defendable space around their home, and
register with the Vic Emergency app for alerts.

Source: https://www.cfa.vic.gov.au

# 7.11 FLOOD RISK

Significant waterways in the municipality (Toolern Creek, Kororoit
Creek, Djerriwarrh Creek, Werribee River tributaries, the Melton
Reservoir) can contribute flood risk in events. Melbourne Water
publishes 1% AEP flood-extent mapping. Properties in flood-prone
areas should have a flood plan and sign up to Vic Emergency alerts.

Source: https://www.melbournewater.com.au/services/apply-permits-and-forms

# 7.12 ROAD SAFETY PROGRAMMING

Council partners with Victoria Police, Department of Transport and
Planning, and the Transport Accident Commission (TAC) on:
- School-road safety (crossing supervisors, School Safe Zones)
- Pedestrian- and cyclist-safety upgrades
- Speed-management (40 km/h treatments on residential streets where
  justified)

Source: https://www.melton.vic.gov.au/Services/Roads-footpaths

# 7.13 FOOTPATHS AND SHARED PATHS

Council maintains local footpaths and shared paths. Report a damaged
footpath via the online form or call (03) 9747 7200.

Source: https://www.melton.vic.gov.au/Services/Roads-footpaths

# 7.14 PARKING ON NATURE STRIP

Parking on the nature strip is generally prohibited under the Road
Safety Road Rules (Rule 197) unless signed otherwise. Enforcement by
Council's Local Laws team.

Source: https://www.vicroads.vic.gov.au

# 7.15 COUNCIL DISASTER RECOVERY

In the recovery phase of a declared emergency, Council operates:
- Municipal Relief Centres (prepared buildings — e.g., leisure centres,
  community halls)
- Relief referrals (temporary accommodation, financial-hardship
  referrals)
- Clean-up assistance coordination

Contact: Council's 24/7 after-hours line (activated in emergencies)
on (03) 9747 7200.

Source: https://www.melton.vic.gov.au/Regulations/Emergency-management

sensory-friendly aquatic space designed for people with sensory
sensitivities, alongside standard aquatic, wellness and program
offerings.
